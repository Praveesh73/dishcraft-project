Main Course


  1. Ingredients:
    • 1 lb ground beef
    • 1 onion, chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
    • 1/4 cup tomato paste
    • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
    • 1 lb spaghetti
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Fresh Parmesan cheese for topping

    Instructions:
    1. Cook spaghetti according to package directions. Drain and set aside.
    2. In a large skillet, cook ground beef over medium heat until browned. Remove excess fat.
    3. Add onion and garlic to the skillet and cook until softened.
    4. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 20 minutes.
    5. Serve the sauce over spaghetti and top with fresh Parmesan.


  2. Ingredients:
    • 4 salmon fillets
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • 1 lemon, sliced
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Fresh dill for garnish (optional)

    Instructions:
    1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
    2. Brush the salmon fillets with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
    3. Place the salmon on the grill, skin-side down. Grill for 4-5 minutes per side or until the salmon is cooked through.
    4. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the salmon and garnish with dill before serving.


  3. Ingredients:
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
      (e.g., canola or peanut oil)
    • 1 medium onion, sliced
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
    • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
    • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
    • 1 cup broccoli florets
    • 1 cup snap peas or green beans
    • 1 medium carrot, julienned
    • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
    • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce (optional)
    • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
    • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water (optional, for thickening)
    • Cooked rice or noodles (for serving)
    • Sesame seeds or chopped green onions (for garnish)

    Instructions:
    1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
    2. Add onion, garlic, and ginger, and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
    3. Add the vegetables starting with the harder ones (e.g., carrots, broccoli) and stir-fry for 3–4 minutes.
    4. Add the softer vegetables (e.g., bell peppers, mushrooms, snap peas) and stir-fry for another 3–4 minutes.
    5. In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, hoisin sauce (if using), and sesame oil. Pour the mixture over the vegetables and toss to coat.
    6. If you prefer a thicker sauce, stir in the cornstarch slurry and cook for 1–2 minutes until the sauce thickens.
    7. Serve over rice or noodles and garnish with sesame seeds or green onions.


  4. Ingredients:
    • 2 large chicken breasts,
      halved lengthwise to make 4 fillets
    • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
    • 2 large eggs, beaten
    • 1 cup breadcrumbs (Panko or Italian-style)
    • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
    • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
    • 1/4 cup olive oil
    • 1 cup marinara sauce
    • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
    • Fresh basil leaves (optional, for garnish)

    Instructions:
    1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
    2. Set up three shallow bowls: one with flour, one with beaten eggs, and one with breadcrumbs mixed with Parmesan cheese, oregano, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
    3. Dredge each chicken fillet in flour, then egg, and finally the breadcrumb mixture, pressing gently to adhere.
    4. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook the chicken fillets for 2–3 minutes per side until golden brown. Transfer to a baking dish.
    5. Spoon marinara sauce over each piece of chicken and top with shredded mozzarella.
    6. Bake in the oven for 15–20 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and the cheese is bubbly and golden.
    7. Garnish with fresh basil leaves, if desired, and serve hot over pasta or with a side salad.
